Introduction to Business Management

A foundational understanding of business management tailored for Computer Science & AI students. It integrates classical management principles with the dynamics of the digital and AI-driven economy, enabling students to comprehend and design business models and strategies in a technological context. This page mirrors the official IE University syllabus; the interactive demos visualise its core quantitative ideas.

Learning objectives

What you should be able to do by the end of the course.

  • Understand foundational principles of management and the role of businesses in modern economies.
  • Analyze and design business models using traditional and modern frameworks.
  • Recognize how AI and digital technologies influence strategy, decision-making, and organizational structure.
  • Assess internal and external environments using tools like SWOT, PESTEL, and Porter's Five Forces.
  • Understand the functional areas of the firm and how they interconnect.
  • Apply concepts through practical projects and real-world case studies.

Program — 5 modules · 30 sessions

The full session-by-session structure from the syllabus. Demos from the home page are cross-referenced where relevant.

Module 1 — Foundations of Business

Sessions 1–2

Evolution of business models, economic systems & market structures, public vs private enterprise.

SESSION 1
Course Introduction + History of Business

Introduce objectives, expectations and evaluation; historical overview from ancient trade to the digital economy.

  • Key business eras: pre-industrial, Industrial Revolution, Management Revolution, Digital Age
  • The role of business in society over time
Bain3,000 Years of Business History · bookSamuelson & Nordhaus, Economics
SESSION 2
Market Structures + Public vs Private Enterprises

Analyze different market structures and ownership models.

  • Perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly
  • Public vs private: efficiency & innovation; public-private partnerships
HBS caseThe Robin Hood Army · bookSamuelson & Nordhaus, Microeconomics

Module 2 — Strategic Thinking & Tools

Sessions 3–10

Strategy fundamentals; SWOT, PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces; Lean Startup & Business Model Canvas; Blue Ocean & AI.

SESSION 3
Strategy Basics: What is Strategy?

Introduce strategic thinking and the role of strategy in business success.

  • Definitions & types; competitive advantage and positioning
  • Corporate, business and functional strategies
tutorialIndustry Evolution · Disruptive Change · Strategy Archetypes
SESSION 4
SWOT, PESTEL & Porter's Five Forces

Learn strategic analysis frameworks for diagnosing businesses.

  • SWOT; PESTEL macro-environmental analysis
  • Five Forces: rivalry, supplier/buyer power, substitutes, new entrants
demoFive forces · SWOT decision (home page) · HBSStrengths Really Weaknesses?
SESSION 5
Blue Ocean Strategy + AI & Strategic Advantage

Innovation-driven strategy and the role of AI in strategic positioning.

  • Value innovation; eliminate-reduce-raise-create grid
  • Red vs blue ocean markets; AI for data-driven advantage
bookKim & Mauborgne, Blue Ocean Strategy · HBRAI-Powered Strategy
SESSION 6
Business Model Canvas + Lean Startup

Understand how to design and iterate business models.

  • BMC components; Lean Startup methodology & MVPs
  • Iteration and pivoting in startups
StrategyzerBMC Explained · bookRies, The Lean Startup · Dropbox MVP
SESSION 7
Value Chain & Business Process Mapping

Understand internal operations and how businesses create value.

  • Porter's Value Chain: primary & support activities
  • Cost drivers, differentiation; process modelling & re-engineering
bookPorter, Competitive Advantage · HubSpotValue Chain Analysis
SESSION 8
Product Life Cycle, BCG Matrix & Growth Strategies

Analyze products and units using life-cycle and portfolio planning frameworks.

  • PLC phases; BCG matrix: Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, Dogs
  • Growth: market penetration, product development, diversification
demoLife cycle · BCG matrix (home page) · BCGGrowth-Share Matrix
SESSION 9 · milestone
Groupwork 1 — Business Model presentations

Present and defend a business-model proposal; peer and instructor feedback.

SESSION 10 · milestone
Half-Term Exam

Module 3 — Organizational Design, Leadership & Governance

Sessions 11–15

Corporate governance, strategy implementation & the 7-S model, structures, culture & responsible AI, leadership.

SESSION 11
Governance, Board & CEO Roles

Fundamentals of corporate governance and how board/CEO relationships shape strategic direction.

  • Board responsibilities; CEO–board–shareholder relationship
PwCThe Governance Divide · HBRRole of Boards in Startups
SESSION 12
Strategy Implementation, Strategic Direction & the 7-S Model

Translate strategy into action by aligning purpose, leadership, systems and structure.

  • Purpose, vision, values, goals; McKinsey 7-S framework
  • Strategic management in dynamic environments
HBSBalanced Scorecard · McKinsey7-S Framework
SESSION 13
Organizational Structures

Understand how organizations are structured to align strategy and execution.

  • Functional, divisional, matrix; team-based & networked organizations
  • Advantages & challenges of each model
HBSNote on Organizational Structure
SESSION 14
Ethics, Culture & Responsible AI

Cultural and ethical responsibilities in global and tech-intensive environments.

  • Organizational culture & Hofstede's dimensions
  • Ethics in management; responsible AI: bias, transparency, accountability
Hofstede6-D model · WEFResponsible AI Principles
SESSION 15
Leadership, Motivation & Decision-Making

The human side of management and effective leadership.

Module 4 — Functional Areas of Business

Sessions 16–22

Marketing, sales, R&D, finance, HR, legal and IT/MIS — and how AI & analytics transform each function.

Sales: Funnels, CRM & KPIs

  • B2B vs B2C; sales funnel stages & KPIs

R&D: Innovation Processes in Tech

How businesses manage research and development.

  • Ideation, prototyping, testing; open innovation
  • Tech trends driving R&D in AI and software
HBRHow Companies Accelerate Innovation · bookKelley, The Art of Innovation
SESSION 19
Finance: Basics, Budgeting & Profitability

Financial decision-making within organizations.

  • Income statements, balance sheets, cash flow
  • Budgeting & variance; profitability metrics & break-even

SESSION 20
HR: People Analytics & Talent Strategy

The evolving role of HR in talent development and analytics.

  • HR analytics and predictive modeling
HBSIs HR the Most Analytics-Driven Function? · bookFournier, The Manager's Path
SESSION 21
Legal Aspects: IP, Data & Compliance

Legal and regulatory issues affecting businesses, especially in tech.

  • IP: patents, trademarks, copyright; data protection & GDPR

SESSION 22

  • Digital infrastructure and transformation
bookLaudon & Brabston, Management Information Systems

Module 5 — Digital Transformation & Globalization

Sessions 23–26

Scaling tech businesses, platform & SaaS economics, the gig economy, internationalization and the investor pitch.

SESSION 23
Scaling Businesses (SaaS, Product-Market Fit)

How startups and tech firms manage rapid growth.

  • Growth vs scaling; SaaS unit economics & customer lifecycle
  • Achieving product-market fit
demoSaaS growth (home page) · bookCroll & Yoskovitz, Lean Analytics
SESSION 24
Platforms, Subscriptions & the Gig Economy

New business models and how technology enables decentralized participation.

  • Subscription & SaaS models; the gig economy

SESSION 25

How businesses scale globally and tailor strategy for international markets.

  • Exporting, franchising, joint ventures, subsidiaries
  • Risk & complexity; entry modes and positioning abroad
tutorialInternationalization Strategies · BCGWhen Asset Light Is Right

Structure a compelling business plan and communicate it through a professional investor pitch.
